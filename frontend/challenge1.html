Caesar Cipher Explained

The Caesar cipher is a type of substitution cipher where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a certain number of places down or up the alphabet. It is named after Julius Caesar, who used it to communicate with his officials.

In this challenge, a shift of 3 was used, so each letter was shifted 3 positions to the right in the alphabet.
